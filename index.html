Breaking News: First reported case of Ebola in Pinconning Michigan

Wednesday October 22nd, 2014

By: James E. Fields

Mid-Michigan Ebola Outbreak!

Pinconning Michigan has become the location of the most recent Ebola outbreak in the United States. Pinconning, Michigan is a small town north of the Tri-City area and is best known for its famous cheese. Officials are reporting that a local farmer and cheese maker has contracted the disease and has been transferred to Atlanta, Georgia for further treatment. While investigations are still underway, researchers at the Center for Disease Control believe that Ebola virus has recently mutated to also effect cattle. Officials are theorizing that the Pinconning farmer might have contracted the disease from a hepher that they had recently purchased in Texas. During late September the hepher arrived to the homestead of the local farmer. Reports suggest that the farmer may have consumed cheese that was produced by the hepher, causing the consumption of the Ebola virus.

A few members of the farmers family are under close watch as they might have contracted the disease as well. The entire farm area and the surrounding farm areas are under strict quarantine as the Michigan State Government tries to prevent a wide scale outbreak. The Center for Disease Control urges anyone in the area to contact them or local authorities is they think that they might be showing common symptoms of Ebola. Please remember that Ebola has a 90% mortality rate.


Common Symptoms Include

After an incubation period of two to 21 days:

  • Abrupt fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle Aches
  • Sore Throat
  • Weakness

Progression of Ebola Symptoms Includes:

  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Stomach Pain
  • Hiccups
  • Rash


If you live in the area Officials are urging you to stay home and not to come near the affected quarantined area. Officials are also urging local farmers to keep a diligent watch over their cattle over the following weeks to ensure that the disease hasn't spread to other herds in the area. If you believe that a member of your cattle has become ill with the Ebola virus please contact the authorities for assitance. We wish the best of luck to Pinconning, Michigan over the following weeks.
